The real science fueling the saga

Rhode Island estuaries, algae lab work, and Cretaceous-era field notes all bleed into Sky City and the Signals dispatches. This page is the lab journal—showing the freshwater and saltwater studies, oyster resilience research, and paleontology references that keep the fiction grounded.

Field research

Rhode Island estuaries & lab benches

My master’s thesis focused on oysters and estuary resilience around Rhode Island. I ran freshwater and saltwater fish surveys, monitored algae blooms from Block Island to Narragansett Bay, and documented how cell walls react under pressure. Those data sets became the technical backbone of Thal estuaries, bonded-creature biology, and the filtration rigs you meet in Sky City.

Estuaries · Thesis

Oysters vs. Sky City runoff

Real sediment load tests around Rhode Island became the model for how Sky City poisons the rivers below. Igzier’s exile storyline taps directly into these datasets, forcing tech fixes that mirror actual mitigation strategies.

Fish surveys · Fieldwork

Freshwater & saltwater scouting

Species logged off Rhode Island—striped bass, fluke, alewife—inform the hybrid creatures inside Tethys. The Signals dispatches borrow entire behaviors from those nocturnal runs.

Algae · Cell walls

Molecular scaffolding

Cell wall stress tests become the blueprint for bonded-creature regeneration, and the way Watchers weave bioelectric nets during ashstorms.
